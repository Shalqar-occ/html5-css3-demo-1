<h1>1. Computer virus</h1>
<h3> Definition</h3>
<p>Not to be confused with computer worm or Trojan horse (computing).<br><br>
A computer virus is a type of computer program that, when executed, replicates <br>itself by modifying other computer programs and inserting its own code.<br> When this replication succeeds, the affected areas are then said to be "infected" <br>with a computer virus.</p>
<p><b>What are the signs of a computer virus?</B></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Frequent pop-up windows:</b> Pop-ups might encourage you to visit unusual sites.<br> Or they might prod you to download antivirus or other software programs.</li>
<li><b>Changes to your homepage:</b> Your usual homepage may change to another website,<br> for instance. Plus, you may be unable to reset it.</li>
<li><b>Mass emails being sent from your email account:</b> A criminal may take control<br> of your account or send emails in your name from another infected computer.</li>
<li><b>Frequent crashes:</b> A virus can inflict major damage on your hard drive.<br> This may cause your device to freeze or crash. It may also prevent your device <br>from coming back on.</li>
<li><b>Unusually slow computer performance:</b> A sudden change of processing<br> speed could signal that your computer has a virus.</li>
